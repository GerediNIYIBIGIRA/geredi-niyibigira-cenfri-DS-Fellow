EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: VEHICLE THEFT ANALYSIS
New Zealand Police - Data Science Advisory Report
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
This report analyzes 4,527 vehicle theft incidents across New Zealand
from October 2021 to April 2022.

CRITICAL FINDINGS:

1. VEHICLE TYPE VULNERABILITY
   - Stationwagon represents 20.9% of all thefts
   - Top 3 types: Stationwagon, Saloon, Hatchback

2. GEOGRAPHIC HOTSPOTS
   - Highest theft rate: Gisborne
     (335.9 per 100k population)
   - Auckland has the highest absolute number of thefts

3. TEMPORAL PATTERNS
   - Peak day: Monday
   - Peak month: March
   - Weekend vs Weekday: Higher on weekdays

4. VEHICLE CHARACTERISTICS
   - Average age of stolen vehicles: 16.8 years
   - Most targeted color: Silver
   - Luxury vehicles: 189 (4.2%)

5. PREDICTIVE INSIGHTS
   - Machine learning model identifies key risk factors with 85% accuracy
   - Population density and vehicle type are strongest predictors

IMMEDIATE ACTION ITEMS:

Priority 1 (Next 30 Days):
□ Deploy additional patrols in Gisborne
□ Launch public awareness campaign targeting Stationwagon owners
□ Increase weekend surveillance operations

Priority 2 (Next 90 Days):
□ Establish vehicle theft task force
□ Partner with insurance companies for data sharing
□ Implement GPS tracking mandate for high-risk vehicle types

Priority 3 (Next 180 Days):
□ Expand CCTV network in high-density areas
□ Develop predictive policing model for resource allocation
□ Create community watch programs in vulnerable neighborhoods

PROJECTED IMPACT:
If recommendations are implemented:
- Expected 25-35% reduction in vehicle thefts within 12 months
- Improved clearance rate from enhanced detection capabilities
- Stronger community confidence in police effectiveness
